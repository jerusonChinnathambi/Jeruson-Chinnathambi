📊 Presentation
Apple's Just-In-Time: ARM Chip Operations & Logistics
Operations & Logistics California Baptist University
Jeruson's Role: Co-Presenter & Analyst

A data-driven analysis of Apple Inc.'s Just-In-Time supply chain strategy, examining how COVID-19 disruptions exposed critical vulnerabilities in Apple's 90%+ Asia-concentrated supplier base. The team used Excel, Power BI, Tableau, and SQL to analyze financial reports, sustainability data, geopolitical risk, and labor data — then recommended a restructured global supplier distribution strategy.

  • Analyzed Apple's supply chain shift from 2020 to 2025: market share grew from 20% to 27% of the smartphone sector
  • Examined COVID impact: online work grew 12.2%; 1.6B students shifted online — spiking device demand against constrained supply
  • Identified single-supplier risk: over 90% of Apple's supplier facilities concentrated in Asia (Taiwan semiconductor dependency)
  • Proposed diversification: reduce Asia from 90% → 40%; expand Africa to 15%, Central America to 10%, North America to 10%
  • Reviewed Apple's supplier responsibility: 98% compliance with 60-hour work-week, 95% violations corrected within 90 days
  • Used analytical tools: Excel, Power BI, Tableau, SQL across financial reports and ESG/labor datasets
